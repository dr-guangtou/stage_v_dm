## Phase 1: SHMR Model Validation (`outputs/phase1/`)

### `phase1/shmr_validation.png`
**Created:** 2026-03-19

Stellar-to-halo mass ratio M*/Mh as a function of halo mass log10(Mh/Msun) at four redshifts (z = 0.0, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0), using the Moster+2013 parameterization with fiducial parameters from their Table 1.

The x-axis spans log10(Mh) = 10 to 15 (in solar masses). The y-axis shows the dimensionless ratio M*/Mh from 0 to 0.07. Each curve represents a different redshift, color-coded: z=0.0 (blue), z=0.5 (orange), z=1.0 (green), z=2.0 (red).

At z=0, the SHMR peaks at M*/Mh = 0.038 (3.8% efficiency) at log Mh = 11.8, annotated with an arrow. This is the characteristic halo mass where galaxies convert baryons to stars most efficiently. Below the peak, the ratio falls as a power law with slope set by beta_0 = 1.376 (feedback from supernovae suppresses star formation in low-mass halos). Above the peak, it falls with slope gamma_0 = 0.608 (AGN feedback suppresses star formation in massive halos).

With increasing redshift: (1) the peak amplitude decreases monotonically (0.038 at z=0 to 0.019 at z=2), reflecting lower integrated star formation efficiency at earlier times; (2) the peak shifts to higher halo mass (log Mh = 11.8 at z=0 to 12.4 at z=2), because the characteristic mass M1 evolves as log M1(z) = 11.59 + 1.195 * z/(1+z); (3) the low-mass slope flattens at higher z (beta decreases from 1.38 to 0.83).

**Purpose:** Validates that the SHMR model implementation reproduces expected physical behavior before use in the halo model integrals. Acceptance test AT-1 (round-trip SHMR inversion to <0.01 dex) is verified separately in the terminal output.

### `phase1/scatter_vs_log_Mh.png`
**Created:** 2026-03-19

**Axes:** x = log₁₀(Mh/M☉) [10, 15], y = σ(log M* | Mh) [dex] [0.05, 0.50].

**Content:** Compares the constant SHMR scatter (σ = 0.15 dex, gray dashed line) with the Cao & Tinker (2020) mass-dependent parameterization (blue solid line). The mass-dependent model uses a tanh transition: σ(Mh) = σ_high + σ_rise × [1 − tanh((log Mh − log Mh,break) / δ)], with fiducial values σ_high = 0.18 dex, σ_rise = 0.10 dex, log Mh,break = 12.0, δ = 0.4 dex. A light blue shaded band shows ±30% variation in σ_rise.

**Physical interpretation:** The scatter increases from ~0.18 dex at cluster masses (Mh > 10^13) to ~0.38 dex at dwarf-galaxy masses (Mh < 10^11). The transition occurs near Mh ~ 10^12 M☉, the characteristic mass where the SHMR peaks. This reflects the increasingly stochastic galaxy formation physics in low-mass halos.

**Purpose:** Validates the Cao & Tinker (2020) scatter implementation and visualizes its behavior relative to the constant-scatter baseline used in earlier phases.

### `phase1/shmr_with_scatter.png`
**Created:** 2026-03-19

**Axes:** Top panel: x = log₁₀(Mh/M☉) [10, 15], y = log₁₀(M*/M☉) [6.5, 12.5]. Bottom panel: same x, y = M*/Mh (log scale) [10⁻⁵, 0.2].

**Content:** Two-panel figure showing the fiducial Moster+2013 SHMR at z = 0 (blue), z = 0.5 (orange), and z = 1.0 (red). Shaded bands show ±1σ(Mh) using the Cao & Tinker (2020) mass-dependent scatter, which is redshift-independent. A gray dotted line marks M* = Mh (100% efficiency). The bottom panel shows M*/Mh (star formation efficiency) on a log scale, with a gray band marking the expected 2–6% peak range.

**Physical interpretation:** The SHMR steepens and shifts at higher z — galaxies form less stellar mass per unit halo mass at earlier times. The scatter bands are visibly wider at low halo masses (σ ~ 0.38 dex at Mh ~ 10^{10.5}) than at high masses (σ ~ 0.18 dex at Mh > 10^{13}). In the efficiency panel, the peak sits at Mh ~ 10^{12} M☉ at all redshifts, consistent with the known "pivot mass." The broader scatter at low mass creates a large uncertainty envelope on the efficiency, reflecting the stochastic nature of dwarf-galaxy formation.

**Purpose:** Illustrates the full fiducial SHMR model used in the forecast, combining the Moster+2013 mean relation with redshift evolution and the Cao & Tinker (2020) mass-dependent scatter.

## Phase 2: Halo Model Predictions (`outputs/phase2/`)

### `phase2/hod_occupation.png`
**Created:** 2026-03-19

Two-panel figure showing the halo occupation distribution (HOD) at z = 0.3 for four stellar mass bins: [9.5, 10.0], [10.0, 10.5], [10.5, 11.0], and [11.0, 11.5] in log10(Msun).

**Left panel — Central occupation N_cen(Mh):** Mean number of central galaxies per halo (0 to 1) as a function of log10(Mh). Each M* bin produces a bell-shaped curve centered at the halo mass where the mean SHMR hits the bin center. The width is set by the SHMR scatter σ(Mh). Peak N_cen ~ 0.90 for all bins.

**Right panel — Satellite occupation N_sat(Mh):** Mean number of satellite galaxies per halo (log scale, 10^{-5} to 100) vs log10(Mh). Uses a Leauthaud+2011 prescription with fixed parameters (alpha_sat = 1.0, M1_sat/Mmin = 17).

**Purpose:** Validates that the HOD derived from the SHMR produces physically reasonable occupation functions before computing ΔΣ and clustering observables.

### `phase2/delta_sigma_model.png`
**Created:** 2026-03-19

Galaxy-galaxy lensing signal ΔΣ(R) as a function of projected physical radius R (Mpc) at z = 0.3, for four stellar mass bins and two single-NFW reference halos. Both axes are logarithmic. The x-axis spans R = 0.1 to 30 physical Mpc. The y-axis shows ΔΣ in Msun/pc^2 from 0.01 to 500.

**Purpose:** Validates the bin-averaged lensing signal against known single-halo profiles. These model predictions are the "signal" in the Fisher matrix; the covariance module provides the "noise."

### `phase2/delta_sigma_1h_2h_decomposition.png`
**Created:** 2026-03-20

**Left panel:** ΔΣ(R) decomposition for three stellar mass bins at z = 0.3. Solid lines show the total signal (1-halo + 2-halo), dashed lines show the 1-halo NFW contribution only, dotted lines show the 2-halo term (b_eff × ΔΣ_matter). The 1-halo term dominates at R < 1 Mpc; the 2-halo term takes over at R ~ 1–3 Mpc depending on halo mass.

**Right panel:** Fraction of the total ΔΣ contributed by the 2-halo term, as a function of R. The crossover (50% line) occurs at R ~ 0.7–1.5 Mpc.

**Purpose:** Validates the NFW + analytic 2-halo implementation and shows the radial regime where each term contributes to the Fisher information.
